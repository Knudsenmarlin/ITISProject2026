Background: Acute ischemic stroke is a leading cause of disability, and early rehabilitation may enhance neurological recovery.

Objective: To assess whether early mobilization within 24 hours of stroke onset improves functional outcomes compared with standard rehabilitation timing.

Methods: This multicenter randomized clinical trial enrolled 240 patients with acute ischemic stroke. Participants were randomized to early mobilization (within 24 hours) or standard rehabilitation initiation (after 48â€“72 hours). Primary outcome was functional independence at 90 days measured by the modified Rankin Scale. Secondary outcomes included length of hospital stay and complication rates.

Results: Early mobilization resulted in higher rates of functional independence at 90 days (61% vs 48%, p = 0.02) and shorter hospital stays. No increase in adverse neurological events was observed.

Conclusions: Early mobilization following acute ischemic stroke improves functional recovery without compromising safety.
